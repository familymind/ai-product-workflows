## 2. Screener questionnaire

Use to recruit participants who match the primary familymind user profile.

### Core screener questions

1. Do you currently work full-time or part-time? [Yes / No / Self-employed]
2. Do you have children under 12 living in your household? [Yes / No]
3. Does your partner also work full-time or part-time? [Yes / No / No partner]
4. How would you describe the division of household and family coordination tasks between you and your partner? [Mostly me / Roughly equal / Mostly my partner / I handle it alone]
5. How often do you feel overwhelmed by the number of family logistics you need to keep track of? [Rarely / Sometimes / Often / Almost always]
6. Have you used any app or tool to help organize your family life in the last 6 months? [Yes — which? / No]

**Ideal participant:** works, has children under 12, partner also works, carries a disproportionate share of coordination, sometimes or often overwhelmed.

## 4. Task scenario templates

Tasks should feel realistic, use natural language, and never hint at the correct solution.

### Good task structure
"Imagine you've just gotten home and you remember you have [real family coordination problem]. Show me what you would do."

### Task quality check
- Does the task reflect something a real user would actually need to do?
- Does the task reveal a specific product assumption you need to test?
- Is the task free from hints about the expected interaction path?
- Can the task be completed (or failed) in a way that gives you useful data?

### Examples for familymind
- "You and your partner both have meetings tomorrow morning and need to sort out who handles school pickup. Show me how you'd handle that using familymind."
- "You've had a stressful week and you're not sure if you missed anything for the kids. Show me how you'd check."
- "Your partner hasn't done much of the planning lately and you want to share the load. Show me how familymind could help with that."
