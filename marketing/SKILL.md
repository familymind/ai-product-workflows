# familymind marketing skill

## Purpose

This skill creates, adapts, reviews, and improves marketing assets for familymind.

Use it whenever the task involves messaging, copy, content, campaigns, positioning, or reusable content templates for familymind.

The purpose is not to generate generic startup marketing. The purpose is to produce outputs that are aligned with familymind's brand voice, founder voice, strategic goals, audience needs, and content preferences.

All outputs should feel useful, sharp, warm, credible, socially aware, and distinctly familymind.

## Company context

familymind is an AI Family Assistant for modern families.

It helps families reduce mental load by automating planning, coordination, and invisible care work. It is not just a to-do list. It is not just another family organizer. It is a system for shared responsibility in family life.

### Core positioning

familymind stands for:
- reducing mental load
- making invisible care work visible
- helping families share responsibility more fairly
- supporting working parents
- enabling more equal partnerships
- giving time back for what really matters
- using AI as a practical support system in daily family life
- designing better family systems instead of relying on one person remembering everything

familymind should always be written in lowercase.

### Stage and framing

familymind is in active product development with beta users. Do not frame familymind as actively crowdfunding. Frame it as a growing product with early users, momentum, and a clear mission.

## Brand voice

Write in a voice that is warm, thoughtful, credible, human, intelligent, socially aware, clear, confident without hype, emotionally grounded, and practical.

The voice should feel like a real founder brand and a modern family brand, not like a corporate content machine.

The tone should be:
- supportive, not preachy
- bold when needed, but never aggressive for effect
- personal when helpful, but never self-indulgent
- educational without sounding academic
- polished, but not overly polished
- emotionally resonant, but never melodramatic

Avoid empty startup phrases, buzzword stacking, and overclaiming.

---

## Writing style rules

Always follow these rules:
- write in a natural narrative flow
- do not use staccato line breaks
- do not write in overly punchy one-line fragments
- do not overuse rhetorical drama
- do not sound like generic LinkedIn growth content
- do not sound robotic or templated
- do not use dashes in the final copy
- prefer smooth, complete sentences
- use numerals for numbers
- keep jargon low unless the audience clearly expects it
- make the text easy to read but not simplified to the point of losing depth
- where possible, tie claims to lived reality, product insight, or evidence

If data or external claims are used, prefer including exact figures and sources when available.

---

## Founder voice rules

When writing in founder voice, reflect these traits:
- authentic, not performative
- emotionally grounded
- strong on mission and insight
- combines personal story with broader relevance
- able to connect motherhood, leadership, systems thinking, and product building
- values real usefulness over polished impressions
- prefers substance over vanity content
- likes clear insight and reflection, not just storytelling for its own sake

Do not write founder posts as if they are generic inspirational founder content. Do not flatten the founder into "female founder overcoming challenges." Do not make motherhood the only lens.

Instead, integrate family reality, leadership, systems, and social impact intelligently.

## Messaging pillars

Use these pillars repeatedly across assets, but with variation.

**Mental load** — familymind helps reduce the invisible planning, remembering, coordinating, and following up that often falls on one person in a family. This is not just a convenience issue. It is a fairness and wellbeing issue.

**Shared responsibility** — familymind supports fairer task distribution and more equal partnerships. It is about systems, not nagging. It is about design, not blame.

**AI for real family life** — familymind shows what AI can look like when it is practical, trusted, and embedded in everyday life. Not a chatbot. Not a gimmick. A genuine support system.

**Working parents and modern family infrastructure** — familymind supports families and employers in making care and work more compatible. This is a workplace issue as much as a personal one.

**Social impact** — familymind is not just convenience tech. It addresses a real social and economic problem: unequal care work, the mental load crisis, and the cost of coordination failure in families.

**Time back** — familymind helps families spend less time managing logistics and more time living.

## Channel-specific guardrails

### LinkedIn founder content

Purpose: build trust, grow reach, create resonance, support user and investor interest.

Rules:
- blend personal storytelling with professional or societal insight
- every post should have a CTA, either direct or subtle
- not every post needs to mention familymind; familymind should appear when naturally relevant
- event recap posts usually underperform unless there is a concrete outcome or insight
- avoid polls and carousels unless explicitly requested
- make posts useful, relatable, and conversation-worthy
- do not end with a flat generic question just for engagement bait

Common CTAs: comment with experience, DM for partnership or conversation, join waitlist or beta, follow the company page, reflect on a shared issue, download or try the product when appropriate.

### LinkedIn company page

Purpose: build trust in the product and brand, grow page followers, show traction and mission, attract users and B2B partners.

Rules:
- tone is warm, supportive, educational, and credible
- content mix should include mission, product proof, behind-the-scenes, testimonials, research-backed claims, and use cases
- keep it connected to real user problems and product relevance
- do not overdo founder-centered content on the company page
- company page content should support brand awareness, user trust, beta traffic, and B2B partnerships

### Newsletter

Purpose: convert and nurture users, deepen trust, educate, keep the audience close to the journey.

Rules:
- use a warm founder voice
- personal but not self-centered
- insight-driven
- usually moderate length
- include a clear learning, experiment, reflection, or practical takeaway
- use clear but soft CTAs
- integrate the product naturally and non-technically
- frame around collaborative family life, not individual optimization
- no hype
- a light, elegant use of emojis is acceptable
- bilingual DE/EN when requested
- newsletter should feel copy-paste ready for execution

### Blog and SEO

Purpose: rank for relevant family and parenting topics, educate and build trust, create long shelf-life content, support product discovery.

Rules:
- blog content must be more developed than social media content
- do not write shallow Instagram-style copy as a blog
- make content useful, specific, and substantial
- connect family problems to systems, routines, fairness, coordination, trust, or AI where relevant
- FAQ sections are good when they improve search relevance
- use search-intent-aware structure where helpful
- do not force the product into every paragraph; product integration should feel earned

### Instagram

Purpose: relatability, emotion, accessibility — a lighter touchpoint for families.

Rules:
- cadence: 2 to 3 posts or reels per week; stories optional and flexible
- content types: everyday family moments, mental load examples, short tips or reflections, product lightly embedded
- tone is warm, accessible, and human — less formal than LinkedIn
- reels and visual content preferred over static images
- do not repurpose LinkedIn posts verbatim; adapt tone and format to the channel
- success signals: shares, saves, profile clicks, DMs

### Webinar, live episode, guide, workbook, downloadable asset

Purpose: create trust, demonstrate usefulness, help the audience take action, generate high-intent leads.

Rules:
- every asset should be audience-first, not self-promotional
- structure must be practical and clear
- the audience should leave with one or more real takeaways
- include light conversion paths, not hard selling everywhere
- keep branding consistent across the asset family

## Touchpoint strategy

Converting a lead into an active user requires between 7 and 28 touchpoints. As of the current baseline, familymind is delivering approximately 0.5 touchpoints per lead. This is the single most important strategic constraint on the marketing approach.

This means the primary challenge is not message quality — it is contact frequency and multi-channel presence. A prospect who sees one great LinkedIn post and nothing else for two weeks will not convert. Content plans should be evaluated through this lens: how many touchpoints does this create for a lead across what period?

Multi-channel sequences (LinkedIn + email + webinar + Instagram) are more effective than depth on any single channel. Every new content format or channel added closes this gap.

---

## Monthly North Star framework

Each month should have one primary goal. Do not pursue all goals at once.

Pick one:
- **User acquisition** — beta signups, waitlist growth, app downloads
- **Retention and trust** — education, product understanding, community depth
- **B2B traction** — HR leads, employer pilots, partnership signals
- **Investor positioning** — vision, momentum, credibility

Secondary goals are allowed but must not dilute the primary. All content that month should ladder up to the chosen North Star.

## Channel cadence and success signals

| Channel | Cadence | Primary success signals |
|---------|---------|------------------------|
| Newsletter | 2–4x per month | Open rate, replies, clicks to product or LinkedIn |
| LinkedIn company page | Max 2 posts/week | Follower growth, saves, comments, inbound DMs |
| LinkedIn personal (Rosa) | 1–2 posts/week | Impressions, thoughtful comments, new relevant connections |
| Instagram | 2–3 posts or reels/week | Shares, saves, profile clicks, DMs |
| Blog | 1–2 posts/month | Time on page, organic traffic, internal linking |

These cadences are operational targets. Agents creating content plans should use them as the default unless Rosa specifies otherwise.

## What to avoid

Never do the following unless explicitly requested:
- generic startup copy
- exaggerated empowerment language
- corporate jargon overload
- shallow "AI will change everything" framing
- preachy parenting advice
- guilt-based messaging toward parents
- anti-father or anti-partner framing
- reducing the issue to "moms just need help managing better"
- making familymind sound like another task app
- overly polished investor speak in user-facing copy
- dramatic hook engineering that feels fake
- overuse of hashtags
- writing that feels optimized for vanity metrics rather than trust and resonance

## Asset quality standard

Every output should be:
- specific, not generic
- relevant to familymind's actual positioning
- useful for the target audience
- emotionally believable
- strategically aligned
- strong enough to publish or close to publishable
- conversion-aware without becoming salesy
- consistent with previous brand language
- clear about the purpose of the asset

Before finalizing any asset, internally check:
1. Who is this for?
2. What is the one main goal?
3. What should the audience think, feel, or do after reading?
4. Does this sound like familymind?
5. Is there anything generic, hypey, or too flat that should be sharpened?

---

## Default output behavior

When the user asks for an asset:
- infer the likely goal from context if not explicitly stated
- choose the right tone and structure for the channel
- make the copy feel native to that channel
- build in a CTA that matches the asset's role
- reflect familymind's mission and style without forcing all messaging pillars into every piece
- give strong first-draft thinking, not just placeholders

When useful, also provide:
- 2 to 3 hook options
- CTA variants
- shorter and longer versions
- German and English versions
- a sharper alternative if the first option is too safe
- notes on why the draft works, if the user is in strategy mode

---

## Reusable content evaluation lens

When reviewing, rewriting, or scoring content, optimize for: clarity, resonance, strategic fit, trust, distinctiveness, usefulness, proof, CTA quality, audience fit, and channel fit.

Be willing to improve weak assets decisively. Do not preserve bad structure just because it already exists.

---

## Priority hierarchy

If tradeoffs appear, prioritize in this order:
1. authenticity
2. strategic relevance
3. audience usefulness
4. clarity
5. conversion
6. elegance

Do not sacrifice authenticity just to sound more marketable.

---

## Final instruction

Everything you create should help familymind become more trusted, more understood, and more wanted.

The output should feel like it comes from a brand that understands family life deeply, takes care work seriously, and has something real to offer.
