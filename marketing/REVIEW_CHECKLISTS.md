# familymind content review checklists

Use these checklists to evaluate, score, and improve any marketing asset before it is published or handed off. Apply the relevant checklist for the asset type.

---

## Universal quality checklist

Apply to every asset, regardless of type.

### Brand alignment
- [ ] Does this sound like familymind, not like generic startup content?
- [ ] Is the brand voice warm, credible, and human?
- [ ] Is "familymind" written in lowercase throughout?
- [ ] Does the copy avoid buzzword stacking and empty empowerment language?
- [ ] Does it avoid anti-partner or preachy parenting framing?

### Strategic fit
- [ ] Is it clear who this asset is for?
- [ ] Is the one main goal of this asset clear?
- [ ] Does it connect to at least one of the 6 messaging pillars?
- [ ] Is the CTA appropriate for the asset's role and the audience's stage?
- [ ] Does this asset contribute to brand awareness, user growth, B2B interest, or investor confidence?

### Content quality
- [ ] Is the content specific, not generic?
- [ ] Is every claim grounded in reality, evidence, or product insight?
- [ ] Is there a clear takeaway for the reader?
- [ ] Does it avoid overclaiming?
- [ ] Is the length appropriate for the channel?

### Voice and style
- [ ] Does it read in a natural narrative flow, not a list of fragments?
- [ ] Are there any dashes in the final copy that should be removed?
- [ ] Is it free from jargon the audience would not recognize?
- [ ] Does it avoid sounding robotic or templated?
- [ ] Is punctuation and formatting clean?

### Conversion awareness
- [ ] Is there a CTA?
- [ ] Does the CTA feel natural, not forced?
- [ ] Is the asset conversion-aware without being salesy?

## LinkedIn founder post checklist

- [ ] Does the hook open a real tension, insight, or story rather than a generic opener?
- [ ] Is there a clear point of view that goes beyond stating a problem?
- [ ] Does the post develop an idea, not just describe a feeling?
- [ ] Does the personal element connect to something broader?
- [ ] Is familymind included naturally, or omitted where it does not belong?
- [ ] Does the CTA feel earned by the content of the post?
- [ ] Would Rosa actually post this? Does it sound like her?
- [ ] Is it free from inspiration-porn framing or "female founder" flattening?

**Pass criteria:** 7 of 8 checks must pass. Revise if the hook is generic or the CTA feels unearned.

---

## LinkedIn company page post checklist

- [ ] Is the opening statement specific and relevant?
- [ ] Does it offer value to the reader, not just promote the brand?
- [ ] Is it grounded in a real product insight, user truth, or mission statement?
- [ ] Is the tone warm and credible, not corporate?
- [ ] Is the CTA relevant and low-pressure?
- [ ] Does it avoid being too founder-centric?

**Pass criteria:** 5 of 6 checks must pass.

---

## Newsletter checklist

- [ ] Does the subject line make someone want to open the email?
- [ ] Does the opening feel personal and real, not like a broadcast?
- [ ] Is there a clear insight, learning, or reflection at the center?
- [ ] Does the practical takeaway give the reader something they can use?
- [ ] Is the product integrated naturally, not forced in?
- [ ] Is the CTA soft and appropriate for the relationship stage?
- [ ] Does the sign-off feel human?
- [ ] Is it free from hard-sell language anywhere in the email?

**Pass criteria:** 7 of 8 checks must pass.

---

## Blog post checklist

- [ ] Is the title specific, useful, and search-intent aware?
- [ ] Does the intro establish audience relevance within the first 2 sentences?
- [ ] Is the content substantially developed, not just social-post depth?
- [ ] Are the subheadings meaningful and navigable?
- [ ] Does the article include at least one specific, grounded insight or piece of evidence?
- [ ] Is the product integration earned and not forced?
- [ ] Is the conclusion clear and does it leave the reader with a takeaway?
- [ ] Would this article be useful to a parent who found it via search?

**Pass criteria:** 7 of 8 checks must pass.

---

## Landing page checklist

- [ ] Is the headline specific and clear (not clever at the expense of clarity)?
- [ ] Does the problem section feel real and recognizable to the audience?
- [ ] Is the solution framing confident without overclaiming?
- [ ] Is there a clear primary CTA above the fold?
- [ ] Are benefit statements framed around the user, not just the product?
- [ ] Are trust signals or social proof included where relevant?
- [ ] Is the page free from competing CTAs that dilute focus?
- [ ] Does the copy feel like familymind, not like a generic SaaS landing page?

**Pass criteria:** 7 of 8 checks must pass.

---

## Webinar or live session checklist

- [ ] Is the session title specific enough to attract the right audience?
- [ ] Is the audience promise clear and credible?
- [ ] Does the run of show leave room for interaction?
- [ ] Is there a clear takeaway the audience will leave with?
- [ ] Is the CTA for after the session relevant and low-pressure?
- [ ] Would Rosa feel comfortable delivering this?
- [ ] Is the structure tight enough to fit the time slot without rushing?

**Pass criteria:** 6 of 7 checks must pass.

## Scoring scale

Use this scale when asked to score an asset rather than approve or reject it.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9 to 10 | Publish-ready. Strong voice, clear goal, well-executed. |
| 7 to 8 | Nearly there. One or two things to sharpen before publishing. |
| 5 to 6 | Solid foundation but needs meaningful revision. Flag specific issues. |
| 3 to 4 | Concept is present but execution is weak. Significant rewrite needed. |
| 1 to 2 | Does not meet brand or strategic standards. Rebuild from brief. |

---

## Common failure modes to flag

Flag the following immediately during any review:

- **Generic opener** — the hook could apply to any startup or founder
- **Mission drift** — the asset sounds like a different brand
- **Overclaiming** — a claim cannot be supported by evidence or product reality
- **CTA mismatch** — the CTA does not match the asset's purpose or the audience's stage
- **Tone inconsistency** — part of the asset sounds warm and part sounds corporate
- **Feature dump** — the copy lists features without explaining why they matter
- **Self-indulgence** — the founder voice is too inward without connecting to the audience
- **Flatness** — the asset is technically correct but has no energy or distinctiveness
