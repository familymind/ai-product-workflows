# familymind asset templates

This file defines the default output structure for each marketing asset type. Use these templates as scaffolding. Always adapt them to the specific goal, audience, and context of the request.

---

## 1. LinkedIn founder post

### Use when
Creating a post for Rosa's personal LinkedIn profile.

### Default output structure
1. hook (1 to 2 sentences, opens a tension, insight, or story)
2. body (2 to 4 short paragraphs developing the idea)
3. CTA (direct or subtle, matched to the post's purpose)

### Hook options
Always provide 2 to 3 alternative hooks so the founder can choose the angle that feels most authentic.

### Recommended length
150 to 300 words for most posts. Long-form is acceptable for high-insight pieces.

### Good qualities
- opens with something real, not a performance
- develops an idea with insight, not just a feeling
- ties personal experience to a broader pattern or problem
- CTA feels natural, not bolted on
- sounds like a person, not a brand

### Avoid
- "Here's what I learned about..." openers that are overused
- listing lessons without context
- ending with a hollow question just to drive comments
- making every post about familymind directly

## 2. LinkedIn company page post

### Use when
Creating a post for the familymind company page.

### Default output structure
1. opening statement or insight (1 to 2 sentences)
2. core message with context or evidence (2 to 3 paragraphs)
3. CTA (follow, try, download, or engage)

### Recommended length
100 to 250 words.

### Good qualities
- brand voice is warm and credible
- connected to real user problems or product relevance
- shows mission, proof, or practical value
- CTA matches the post's purpose

### Avoid
- making every post founder-centric
- over-promoting without delivering value
- vague mission statements without grounding

## 3. Newsletter

### Use when
Sending a regular newsletter to subscribers, waitlist members, or beta users.

### Default output structure
1. subject line (2 to 3 options)
2. preview text
3. greeting
4. opening hook or personal reflection (2 to 3 sentences)
5. main body (insight, product update, learning, or story)
6. practical takeaway or reflection for the reader
7. soft CTA
8. sign-off

### Recommended length
300 to 600 words for the body. Shorter is better if the message is sharp.

### Good qualities
- feels like a letter from a founder, not a marketing email
- insight-driven, not just informational
- practical takeaway the reader can actually use
- product mentioned naturally when relevant, not forced
- warm and personal without being self-indulgent

### Avoid
- hard selling
- more than one strong CTA
- long lists of product features
- tone that feels automated or templated

## 4. Blog post

### Use when
Writing an article for the familymind blog or website, or for SEO purposes.

### Default output structure
1. title (2 to 3 SEO-aware options)
2. optional subtitle
3. intro (sets up the problem or tension, draws the reader in)
4. full article with meaningful subheadings
5. FAQ section if it improves search relevance
6. conclusion with takeaway
7. optional meta description
8. optional suggested internal CTA

### Recommended length
700 to 1500 words depending on the topic. Aim for depth over padding.

### Recommended article structure
- clear intro with audience relevance
- problem explanation with real context
- deeper insight or research backing
- practical guidance, examples, or reframing
- natural brand connection where earned
- conclusion with a clear takeaway

### Good qualities
- specific and substantial
- useful and honest
- stronger and more developed than any social post
- readable but not thin
- search-intent aware where relevant

### Avoid
- social post length disguised as a blog
- fluff or filler without practical value
- hard selling in every section
- forcing familymind into every paragraph

## 5. Webinar or live session

### Use when
Preparing a webinar, LinkedIn Live, live event, workshop, or a shared-by-design style conversation.

### Default output structure
1. session title options (3 to 5)
2. recommended title with rationale
3. audience promise (what they will walk away with)
4. target audience
5. run of show with timings
6. main talking points per section
7. CTA for after the session
8. optional follow-up asset idea

### Recommended run of show structure
- opening and welcome (context, speaker intro)
- why this topic matters now
- key themes or questions
- practical examples or audience-relevant content
- Q&A or interactive moment
- wrap-up and takeaway
- CTA

### Good qualities
- clear structure that can be handed off to a speaker
- not over-scripted, but well-planned
- audience gets one or more real takeaways
- brand-aligned but audience-first
- CTA is relevant and low pressure

## 6. Guide, workbook, or downloadable asset

### Use when
Creating a lead magnet, practical guide, or downloadable resource. The goal is usually lead generation, trust building, or audience support.

### Default output structure
1. title (with options)
2. target audience statement
3. promise (what the reader will get)
4. short intro
5. section breakdown with content per section
6. worksheet prompts or exercises if relevant
7. CTA at the end
8. optional follow-up email suggestion

### Good qualities
- immediately useful and actionable
- clear and well-structured
- smart but light branding (familymind present but not dominant)
- high clarity, no jargon overload
- not overloaded with sections

## 7. Landing page

### Use when
Writing a waitlist page, feature page, campaign page, beta sign-up page, or offer page.

### Default output structure
1. headline options (3 to 5)
2. subheadline options
3. recommended version with rationale
4. section-by-section copy:
   - hero section (headline, subheadline, CTA button text)
   - problem section (what is happening for the user)
   - solution section (what familymind does and how)
   - social proof or trust signals (testimonials, beta users, stats if available)
   - feature or benefit highlights (3 to 5 key points)
   - secondary CTA section
   - footer CTA

### Good qualities
- headline is specific and clear, not clever for the sake of it
- problem framing feels real to the audience
- solution framing is confident without overclaiming
- conversion-aware throughout
- consistent with familymind's voice

### Avoid
- feature lists without benefit framing
- jargon the audience does not use
- too many CTAs competing with each other
- any claim that cannot be supported

---

## 8. Email sequence

### Use when
Creating a nurture sequence, onboarding flow, re-engagement series, or campaign email chain.

### Default output structure per email
1. subject line (2 to 3 options)
2. preview text
3. purpose of this email in the sequence
4. body copy
5. CTA
6. notes on timing and trigger if relevant

### Good qualities
- each email has one job
- builds naturally toward conversion without pressure
- voice stays warm and personal across the sequence
- each email could stand alone if read out of sequence

---

## Output format reminder

For any asset:
- deliver copy that is close to or ready to publish
- offer hook or headline variants where relevant
- flag if something needs to be checked or filled in by the team
- include German version if the task is bilingual
- note the strategic rationale if the user is in review or strategy mode
