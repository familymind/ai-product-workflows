Rosa Di Donna is the founder of familymind. Her profile sits at the intersection of:
- leadership and business building
- family life and lived experience with mental load
- systems thinking and product design
- social impact and gender equity
- AI and the future of work

Her LinkedIn presence should reflect all of these dimensions — not reduce her to any single one.

## Founder voice — what it sounds like

Rosa's voice is:
- authentic, not performative
- emotionally grounded without being melodramatic
- strong on insight and clear thinking
- personal when it adds real value, not for the sake of relatability
- able to move between the personal and the structural without losing either
- values-driven but never preachy
- direct and confident without being aggressive

The posts that work best for Rosa:
- start from a real observation or moment
- develop a clear point of view that goes beyond the obvious
- connect the personal to the broader pattern or problem
- leave the reader with something they can take with them

The posts that do not work:
- generic "here is what I learned" listicles
- inspiration-performance without real substance
- "female founder overcoming challenges" framing
- posts that are entirely about familymind as a product
- posts that flatten Rosa into a single dimension (just the mom, just the entrepreneur, just the advocate)

## Content themes for Rosa's profile

These six themes reflect her real areas of authority and should rotate across her posts:

**Mental load and care work** — the structural reality of invisible labor in families. Rosa speaks about this with both personal authority and systemic insight.

**Founder journey** — the real experience of building a startup at the intersection of family life, AI, and social impact. Not the highlight reel — the actual process, the decisions, the tensions.

**AI and product thinking** — what it means to build AI that serves real human needs rather than generating hype. Rosa's product perspective is grounded and distinctive.

**Gender equity and the future of work** — how care work inequality shapes professional careers, and how systemic change requires systemic tools.

**Leadership and systems thinking** — how leaders and founders think about complex, multi-stakeholder problems. Rosa's systems lens applies to family just as much as it applies to organizations.

**Lived family reality** — concrete moments from family life that illuminate larger truths. Used selectively and with purpose, not as a crutch for relatability.

## Post structure guidelines

### Hook
- Opens with a real tension, insight, observation, or moment
- Does not start with "I" as the first word if possible
- Does not use overused openers: "Unpopular opinion:", "Nobody talks about this:", "Hot take:"
- Should create genuine curiosity or recognition — not manufactured drama

### Body
- Develops one idea with real depth
- Connects personal experience to a broader pattern where relevant
- Includes a specific detail or example that makes the post feel real, not generic
- 2–4 short paragraphs for most posts

### CTA
- Feels earned by the content, not bolted on
- Options: ask a genuine question, invite to DM, direct to waitlist, suggest following the company page, invite reflection
- Do not end with a hollow generic question just for engagement

### Length
- Most posts: 150–300 words
- Long-form insight posts: up to 500 words
- Never pad for length

## Tone calibration

| Situation | Tone |
|-----------|------|
| Sharing a personal moment | Warm, grounded, specific |
| Making a structural argument | Clear, confident, evidence-anchored |
| Reacting to news or research | Analytical, with a clear point of view |
| Sharing a product milestone | Honest, proud but not boastful |
| Writing about gender equity | Direct, data-informed, non-preachy |
| Founder reflection | Honest, self-aware, forward-looking |
