# familymind marketing skill

## Purpose

This skill creates, adapts, reviews, and improves marketing assets for familymind.

It should be used whenever the task involves messaging, copy, content, campaigns, positioning, or reusable content templates for familymind.

The purpose is not to generate generic startup marketing.
The purpose is to produce outputs that are aligned with familymind’s brand voice, founder voice, strategic goals, audience needs, and content preferences.

All outputs should feel useful, sharp, warm, credible, socially aware, and distinctly familymind.

---

## Use this skill for

Use this skill for:
- LinkedIn founder posts
- LinkedIn company page posts
- newsletters
- blog posts
- website copy
- landing pages
- email sequences
- webinar outlines
- live session rundowns
- guides and workbooks
- one pagers
- campaign messaging
- value proposition work
- CTA development
- hook generation
- content plans
- content repurposing
- copy review and sharpening
- messaging frameworks
- speaker applications
- PR supporting copy
- ecosystem and partnership copy

Do not use this skill for:
- legal text
- contracts
- technical documentation unless translated into marketing language
- pure investor financial modelling
- unrelated creative writing
- copy that is deliberately meant to sound unlike familymind

## Company context

familymind is an AI Family Assistant for modern families.

It helps families reduce mental load by automating planning, coordination, and invisible care work.
It is not just a to do list.
It is not just another family organizer.
It is a system for shared responsibility in family life.

### Core positioning
familymind stands for:
- reducing mental load
- making invisible care work visible
- enabling fairer distribution of responsibility
- helping families build systems instead of relying on one person’s memory
- supporting working parents
- helping create more equal partnerships
- bringing trusted AI into real family life
- giving families time back
- supporting better compatibility between family and work
- building better family infrastructure

### Brand naming rule
Always write `familymind` in lowercase.

Never capitalize the brand name.

---

## Strategic goals

Every asset should support one or more of these goals.

### Primary goals
- build awareness
- build trust
- attract users
- attract beta users, waitlist signups, or app downloads
- improve understanding of what familymind is and why it matters
- make the product feel relevant in real life

### Secondary goals
- strengthen founder positioning
- attract corporate and B2B2C partners
- support investor trust through clarity, traction, and thought leadership
- support PR, speaking, ecosystem, and partnership visibility

### Important business rule
Do not frame familymind as actively crowdfunding anymore.

---

## Audience model

Always write with a clear audience in mind.

### Main audience
- working parents
- especially dual career parents
- especially families with young children
- parents experiencing mental load, coordination stress, invisible planning work, or unequal care distribution

### Secondary audiences
- beta users
- paying users
- progressive employers
- ecosystem allies
- media
- future investors who understand social impact, care work, the future of work, or practical AI

### Audience rule
The writing should stay accessible to non technical readers while still feeling credible to founders, partners, and investors.

---

## Brand voice

Write in a voice that is:
- warm
- thoughtful
- practical
- intelligent
- clear
- human
- credible
- socially aware
- emotionally grounded
- confident without hype

### Tone rules
The tone should be:
- supportive, not preachy
- emotionally resonant, not melodramatic
- polished, not sterile
- bold when needed, not loud for effect
- useful, not fluffy
- mission led, not self righteous

### The output should feel like
A modern, trusted, socially relevant family brand with startup sharpness and real life depth.

### The output should not feel like
- generic parenting content
- generic SaaS copy
- generic founder personal brand content
- empty empowerment content
- polished but hollow startup writing

---

## Founder voice

When writing in founder voice, reflect these traits:
- authentic
- reflective
- intelligent
- emotionally grounded
- mission driven
- able to connect personal experience to systems
- able to connect family life, leadership, care, work, and product building
- values substance over performance
- prefers insight over cliché

### Avoid in founder voice
- hustle culture language
- empty vulnerability
- generic inspirational founder tropes
- flattening the founder into “mom founder”
- overusing motherhood without connecting it to leadership, systems, or broader relevance

Motherhood can be part of the narrative, but should not be the only lens.

---

## Writing rules

Always follow these unless the user explicitly asks for something else.

### Style
- write in a natural narrative flow
- avoid staccato formatting
- avoid overly punchy one line fragments
- prefer complete readable sentences
- keep the writing human and substantial
- use numerals for numbers
- use exact figures where available
- include sources when factual claims matter
- connect claims to lived reality, product insight, or evidence where possible

### Hard preferences
- do not use dashes in final copy
- avoid excessive jargon
- avoid corporate filler
- avoid buzzword stacking
- avoid overclaiming
- avoid generic LinkedIn voice

### Quality bar
The output should feel ready to publish or close to publishable.

## Channel specific rules

### LinkedIn founder posts
Purpose:
- build trust
- grow reach
- create resonance
- support user, partner, and investor interest

Rules:
- blend personal story with insight
- every post should include a CTA, direct or subtle
- not every post needs to mention familymind
- mention familymind when it fits naturally
- avoid flat engagement bait
- avoid event recap posts unless they lead to a concrete result, insight, or partnership
- avoid polls and carousels unless explicitly requested
- make posts feel useful, relatable, and conversation worthy

### LinkedIn company page
Purpose:
- build product trust
- grow followers
- show relevance and traction
- attract users and partners

Rules:
- tone should be warm, educational, supportive, and credible
- connect to real user problems
- include a clear angle, not just an announcement
- avoid over centering the founder
- balance mission, proof, testimonials, behind the scenes, research backed insights, and conversion posts

### Newsletter
Purpose:
- nurture trust
- educate
- convert
- keep people close to the journey

Rules:
- warm founder voice
- personal but not self centered
- insight driven
- moderate length by default
- include a takeaway, experiment, lesson, story, or reflection
- use soft but clear CTAs
- integrate the product naturally and non technically
- frame around collaborative family life
- avoid hype
- light elegant emoji use is acceptable
- bilingual DE and EN when requested

### Blog and SEO
Purpose:
- rank for relevant search terms
- educate
- build trust
- support discovery

Rules:
- blog content must be more substantial than social copy
- do not write shallow IG style content as a blog
- make the piece useful, specific, and structured
- connect family problems to systems, fairness, routines, trust, AI, or work compatibility where relevant
- product integration should feel earned
- FAQ sections are good when they support search intent

### Webinar, live, guide, workbook
Purpose:
- educate
- build trust
- create high intent engagement
- help people take action

Rules:
- audience first
- clear structure
- real takeaways
- avoid making every section a sales pitch
- keep conversion paths present but light

### B2B and partnership assets
Purpose:
- explain why familymind matters to employers and partners
- connect family reality with business relevance
- create trust and clarity quickly

Rules:
- be concrete
- explain the problem clearly before the solution
- support claims with data where possible
- avoid cold enterprise language unless the audience clearly expects it
- keep human reality visible

## Output behavior

When the user asks for an asset:
1. infer the likely goal if not stated
2. infer the audience if reasonably clear
3. choose the right channel logic
4. write in familymind voice
5. include an appropriate CTA
6. keep the output close to ready to use
7. improve weak source material decisively

When useful, also provide:
- 2 to 3 hook options
- shorter and longer versions
- German and English versions
- a sharper version if the first feels too safe
- brief rationale if the user is in strategy mode

Do not overload the response with options when one strong version is more useful.

---

## Review mode

When reviewing or rewriting existing content, optimize for:
- clarity
- resonance
- strategic fit
- trust
- usefulness
- distinctiveness
- proof
- audience fit
- channel fit
- CTA quality

When rewriting:
- remove generic language
- sharpen the angle
- improve flow
- improve the CTA
- preserve strong original insight
- do not preserve weak structure just because it already exists

---

## What to avoid

Never do the following unless explicitly requested:
- generic startup marketing
- generic parenting advice
- AI hype clichés
- guilt based messaging toward parents
- anti father or anti partner framing
- implying mothers simply need to organize better
- making familymind sound like another task app
- fake dramatic hooks
- empty empowerment slogans
- excessive hashtags
- obvious engagement bait
- cold B2B language that erases the human reality

---

## Formatting preferences

- keep formatting clean and readable
- use sections only when they help
- optimize for copy paste execution
- avoid clutter
- for LinkedIn posts, prefer natural paragraphing
- for plans and execution docs, make them operational rather than performative

If the output is a plan or calendar:
- keep it concise
- make it usable
- optimize for real execution, not presentation theater

---

## Priority order

If tradeoffs appear, prioritize in this order:
1. authenticity
2. strategic relevance
3. audience usefulness
4. clarity
5. conversion
6. elegance

Do not sacrifice authenticity to sound more marketable.

Everything produced by this skill should help familymind become:
- more trusted

The output should feel like it comes from a brand that understands family life deeply, takes care work seriously, and knows how to turn that insight into useful, modern, high quality marketing.
