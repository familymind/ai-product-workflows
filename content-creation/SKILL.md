# SKILL.md — familymind Content Creation Guardrail

## Purpose

This skill governs the creation, review, and quality control of content assets for familymind across all owned and earned channels.

It is a companion to `marketing/SKILL.md`. Where the marketing skill defines strategy, voice, and channel rules, this skill defines the practical execution layer: how to draft, structure, format, and finalize content efficiently without losing brand consistency.

Use this skill together with `marketing/SKILL.md` and `brand/BRAND_CONTEXT.md` for any content creation task.

---

## Scope

Use this skill for:
- drafting content from a brief or request
- adapting existing content to a new channel or audience
- rewriting or sharpening weak first drafts
- building content calendars and execution plans
- formatting content for publication
- bilingual content (DE / EN)
- creating content variations for testing
- repurposing long-form content into shorter formats
- building repeatable content templates for recurring formats

Do not use this skill for: investor communications, sales pitches, financial content, or user testing materials.

---

## How to read an incoming content request

Before drafting, identify:

1. **Channel** — where will this appear? (LinkedIn, newsletter, blog, website, social, other)
2. **Voice** — founder voice or brand voice?
3. **Goal** — what should the reader do, think, or feel after reading?
4. **Audience** — which segment? (working parents, employers, investors, ecosystem)
5. **Length** — short / medium / long?
6. **Language** — German, English, or bilingual?
7. **CTA** — what action is expected?

If any of these are unclear, infer from context and state your assumptions at the top of the draft.

## Drafting principles

### Start with the reader, not the product

Every piece of content should open from the reader's world: their problem, their reality, their question. The product appears when it is earned.

### One post, one job

Do not overload a single piece of content with multiple goals, multiple CTAs, or multiple messages. Choose the most important one and build everything else around it.

### Show, don't just state

Claims land better with evidence, examples, or a concrete scenario. "Working parents feel overwhelmed" is less effective than "a parent who plans school pickups, grocery orders, dentist appointments, and birthday gifts in her lunch break — without a system."

### Structure before prose

For any content longer than 150 words, outline the structure before writing the full draft. Know what each paragraph or section is doing before filling it in.

### Copy-paste ready by default

Unless the task is explicitly a rough draft, deliver content that is ready to post or send with minimal editing. The user should not need to significantly rewrite the output.

## Format rules by channel

### LinkedIn posts (founder or company)
- Natural paragraphing, not bullet lists
- No staccato one-liners stacked on top of each other
- No dashes in the final copy
- CTA at the end, not the beginning
- 150–300 words for most posts; longer for high-insight pieces
- First 1–2 lines must earn the "see more" click

### Newsletter
- Subject line + preview text delivered together
- Body: warm opening, one main insight or update, practical takeaway, soft CTA, human sign-off
- 300–600 words for the body; shorter if the message is sharp
- Emojis acceptable but used lightly and purposefully
- Copy-paste ready, including subject line

### Blog
- Minimum 700 words; 1000–1500 for SEO-targeted pieces
- Title must be specific and search-intent aware
- Subheadings should be meaningful, not decorative
- Product mention earns its place — never forced in every paragraph
- Include FAQ section if it improves relevance for search

### Website / landing page
- Headline: specific and clear, not clever at the expense of clarity
- Every section earns its place
- CTA above the fold and again at the bottom
- Problem section speaks the user's language, not the product's
- Social proof and trust signals where relevant

### Short-form (social captions, snippets, pull quotes)
- Cut to the essential idea
- One clear point, maximum two sentences
- CTA if space allows; omit if it clutters

## Bilingual content rules (DE / EN)

When producing bilingual content:
- Translate the meaning, not the words — idioms and cultural references must be adapted
- German version may have a slightly more formal tone if the audience warrants it
- Keep brand name as "familymind" in both languages
- Messaging pillars translate directly; style and phrasing require adaptation
- Deliver both versions in the same output, clearly labeled

## Content repurposing rules

When repurposing content from one format to another:

| From | To | Rule |
|------|-----|------|
| Blog post | LinkedIn post | Extract the core insight and hook, cut the rest |
| Newsletter | Blog | Develop the key point into a full article; do not just paste the newsletter |
| LinkedIn post | Newsletter | Expand the idea, add context, add a practical takeaway |
| Webinar content | Blog / guide | Restructure around the audience's question, not the session flow |
| Long-form | Social snippet | Isolate the sharpest line or most resonant claim |

Never re-use copy without adapting it to the channel. A LinkedIn post dropped into a newsletter feels like a newsletter written by someone who forgot they were writing a newsletter.

## Content review before delivery

Before delivering any draft, internally check:

- [ ] Does the opening earn attention?
- [ ] Is the one main goal of this piece clear?
- [ ] Is the CTA appropriate and not forced?
- [ ] Does it sound like familymind — warm, credible, human?
- [ ] Is it specific enough to feel real, not generic?
- [ ] Is the format right for the channel?
- [ ] Is it free of dashes in the final copy?
- [ ] Is it free of generic startup phrases?
- [ ] Is it copy-paste ready?

---

## Default output behavior

When generating a content draft:
1. State any assumptions you made (audience, goal, tone)
2. Deliver the primary draft
3. Offer 2–3 headline or hook variants where relevant
4. Offer a shorter and/or longer version if the length is ambiguous
5. Offer a German version if the context is bilingual
6. Flag anything that needs to be verified or filled in by the team

---

## Priority hierarchy

When tradeoffs arise in content creation:
1. Authenticity — does this sound like familymind?
2. Relevance — does this connect to the reader's real situation?
3. Clarity — is it easy to understand?
4. Conversion — does it move the reader toward action?
5. Elegance — does it read well?

Never sacrifice authenticity for any other goal.
