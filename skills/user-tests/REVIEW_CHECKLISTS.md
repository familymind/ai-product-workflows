# REVIEW_CHECKLISTS.md — User Test Assets

Use these checklists before any user test asset is used in a live session or shared as a final readout.

---

## 1. Test plan review checklist

### Scope and focus
- [ ] Is there one clear decision that this test is designed to inform?
- [ ] Would the team act differently depending on the findings?
- [ ] Are the research questions specific enough to be answerable?
- [ ] Is the scope tight enough for the session length?

### Method fit
- [ ] Is the method appropriate for the stage of development?
- [ ] Is moderated or unmoderated the right choice for this question?
- [ ] Is the participant profile clearly defined and realistic to recruit?

### Task quality
- [ ] Are tasks anchored in realistic user scenarios?
- [ ] Do tasks avoid hinting at the expected solution?
- [ ] Do tasks match the research questions?
- [ ] Are tasks realistic enough to produce meaningful behavior?

### Clarity
- [ ] Is the brief short enough to be usable?
- [ ] Could another team member run with it without guessing?

**Pass standard:** A strong test plan makes the decision, method, and success criteria obvious without ambiguity.

---

## 2. Moderator guide review checklist

### Bias control
- [ ] Are questions neutral in tone?
- [ ] Are there any leading phrases that reveal the expected answer?
- [ ] Are there any questions that signal what a "correct" response looks like?

### Usability
- [ ] Can the moderator use this guide live without getting lost?
- [ ] Are probes short and open-ended?
- [ ] Does the guide avoid over-explaining the product during the session?

### Sequence quality
- [ ] Does the guide warm up naturally before tasks?
- [ ] Do tasks progress in a logical order?
- [ ] Does the debrief capture overall understanding without distorting the session?

**Pass standard:** A strong guide helps the moderator observe, not steer.

---

## 3. Task review checklist

### Realism
- [ ] Does the task sound like something a real user would actually try to do?
- [ ] Is the task anchored in a recognizable life situation?
- [ ] Does the task avoid product-specific language that reveals the expected path?

### Signal value
- [ ] Will success or failure on this task actually tell us something useful?
- [ ] Is there a clear observable behavior we are looking for?
- [ ] Does this task connect to a specific product assumption we are testing?

**Pass standard:** A strong task produces observable behavior that can inform a product decision.

---

## 4. Screener review checklist

- [ ] Does the screener identify the right participant profile?
- [ ] Are the questions free from answers that are obviously "correct"?
- [ ] Is the screener short enough that participants will complete it?
- [ ] Are exclusion criteria appropriate and non-discriminatory?

**Pass standard:** A strong screener reliably selects participants who match the primary familymind user profile.

---

## 5. Readout review checklist

### Evidence quality
- [ ] Are observations distinguished from interpretations?
- [ ] Are all claims tied to a specific number of sessions?
- [ ] Are confidence levels labeled for key findings?
- [ ] Is behavioral evidence weighted more heavily than stated preference?

### Decision focus
- [ ] Does the readout connect back to the original decision question?
- [ ] Are the recommended actions specific and prioritized?
- [ ] Is it clear what the team should do next?

### Limitations
- [ ] Are limitations of the test acknowledged?
- [ ] Are there findings that could be misread without the appropriate caveats?

### Brevity
- [ ] Could this readout be usefully shorter?
- [ ] Is every section earning its place?

**Pass standard:** A strong readout gives the team a clear picture of what happened and what to do next, without over-interpreting.

---

## 6. Universal red-flag check

Run before any test asset goes live or gets shared:

- [ ] Is there a leading question that could bias participant responses?
- [ ] Is there a claim in the readout that is not supported by what was observed?
- [ ] Is the confidence level appropriate for the sample size?
- [ ] Does the recommendation go beyond what the test evidence can support?
- [ ] Does the readout bury a critical finding that should have been escalated immediately?

If yes to any of these, revise before proceeding.
